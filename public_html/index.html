RESEARCH

In my thesis work, I developed a client behavior verification technique using symbolic execution. I built a novel server-side system that verifies whether messages from a client could have been generated by a sanctioned software version, without any knowledge of client-side inputs. The technique works by finding a client execution path that "explains" a given message trace. By utilizing execution history to guide our search, I also built a system that is more efficient. And to support verification of cryptographic clients such as OpenSSL, I modified the KLEE symbolic execution engine to support parallel execution.

INTERESTS AND OTHER PROJECTS

  • IMPROV

  • DIY SYNTHESIZERS

    I am interested in "forgotten" forms of musical and aural expression which utilize physical interfaces that bridge the gap between modern electronic music and the analog circuits that first opened ears to new worlds of sound half a century ago. Inspired by vintage hobbyist magazines and found enclosures, I build devices that allow the expression of analog sounds that are both warm and foreign.

  • ELECTRONIC ART

    (In collaboration with Lile Stephens) Using humor to evoke larger issues surrounding society’s use of devices and networks, we are inventing new must have products. By borrowing from the traditional language of advertising and startup culture, we are proposing ‘useless’ products that are can be assembled and adapted from existing devices and software.

    Our first collaboration, E-Bolo was shown at the Spectre Arts Gallery in August, 2016.
